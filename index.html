About the PI

Meet Dr. Qian Zhu: Bridging Computational Biology and Cancer Research

I am an Assistant Professor at Baylor College of Medicine, specializing in computational biology with a focus on integrating spatial transcriptomics and morphological analysis to better understand the tumor microenvironment.

My academic journey began at the University of Ottawa, followed by a Ph.D. from Princeton University, where I developed a strong foundation in computational techniques. My postdoctoral work at Dana-Farber Cancer Institute and Harvard Medical School further refined my expertise, enabling me to contribute significantly to our understanding of complex biological data and its implications in cancer research.

Software

Discover More About Our Software

Giotto
Integrative data analysis and visualization toolkit for spatial transcriptomic data
CUT&RUN Tools
A customizable workflow for performing analyses of CUT&RUN data. Besides reads trimming, alignment, peak calling, it includes de novo motif finding, motif footprinting, and transcription factor binding site predictions.
SEEK
Search-based exploration of expression compendium. A coexpressed gene search and visualization system.
ModSEEK
Model organism extension of SEEK. Covers transcriptome compendia in fly, worm, mouse, zebrafish, and yeast.
smfishHmrf
A Python package for single-molecule FISH machine learning analyses. Includes SVM-based cell type mapping from scRNAseq to seqFISH and spatial pattern mining using hidden Markov random field.
